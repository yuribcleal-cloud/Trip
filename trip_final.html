⛲Trevi Fountain
Go before 9 AM — far fewer crowds
→ Toss a coin with your right hand over your left shoulder — legend says you'll return to Rome. Get a cornetto and cappuccino at the café right behind the fountain.

🏛️Pantheon
€5 entry · book online · 2,000-year-old Roman temple
→ Stand in the centre and look straight up at the oculus — the open hole in the dome that's been there for 2,000 years. On a sunny day a beam of light moves across the interior like a clock.
10:00
🏟️Piazza Navona
Free · Bernini's fountains · terrace coffee
→ Sit at one of the terrace cafés and order a coffee — yes it's touristy but the piazza view is worth every euro. Watch the street artists and portrait painters work.

⚔️Colosseum — Arena FloorBOOK
Full Experience Arena · Gladiator's Gate entry · 2–3 hours
→ When you walk through the Gladiator's Gate onto the arena floor, stop and look up slowly at the 4 levels of arches surrounding you. Take that moment in — 80,000 Romans once watched here.

Cover shoulders + knees · no photos in Sistine Chapel
→ Don't rush to the Sistine Chapel — walk slowly through the Gallery of Maps and Raphael Rooms first. When you enter the Sistine Chapel, find a spot on the benches along the wall, sit, and look up for at least 5 minutes.

Free entry · Michelangelo's Pietà · climb the dome
→ Find the Pietà (Mary holding Jesus) and stand in front of it quietly — it's behind glass but still stunning. Then climb the dome — take the lift halfway and walk the rest. The view from the top is the best in Rome.
13:00

→ Find a spot on the hill's edge, sit down and don't rush this. You can see the Colosseum, the Forum, and the entire Rome skyline bathed in golden light — take it all in slowly.

Jun 14 · Sunday · Rome — Farewell Day 🇮🇹
Sunday + Pope's Angelus at noon = a truly once-in-a-lifetime Roman morning.
09:00
🌳Orange Garden — Aventine Hill
Best free panoramic viewpoint in Rome
→ Find a bench under the orange trees facing St. Peter's dome and just sit quietly — this is one of the most beautiful and peaceful spots in all of Rome. Perfect for a morning coffee from a nearby bar.
09:30
🔑Knights of Malta Keyhole
5-min walk · three countries through one keyhole!
→ Peek through the keyhole in the big wooden door — you'll see Italy, the Vatican and the territory of the Knights of Malta all perfectly aligned with St. Peter's dome at the end. Takes 30 seconds, unforgettable forever.
10:30
🌹Roseto Comunale
Rome's rose garden · free in June · in full bloom!
→ Walk slowly through the terraced rose beds — over 1,100 varieties all blooming in June. It's free, almost nobody is there, and the scent is incredible. Stop at the viewpoint over the Circus Maximus.
12:00
⛪Pope's Angelus — St. Peter's Square
Free · Pope speaks from his window every Sunday at noon
→ Arrive by 11:30 AM and stand in the square looking up at the Pope's window. When he appears the whole crowd goes quiet — it's a genuinely moving moment even if you're not religious. Completely free.
13:30

15:30
🛍️Spanish Steps + Via Condotti
Iconic staircase · luxury shopping street
→ Sit on the steps with a gelato and watch the world go by. Then walk down Via Condotti — even if you're not buying, the windows of Gucci, Bulgari and Valentino are spectacular to browse.
17:00
🎨Via Margutta
Rome's most charming artist street · ivy-covered walls
→ Browse the art studios and small galleries — many artists sell prints and originals. A beautiful place to find a unique souvenir. The street itself is incredibly photogenic with all the ivy.
